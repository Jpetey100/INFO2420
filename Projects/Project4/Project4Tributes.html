Tribute #1 – Brian & Kitty Jones

I'm Brian Jones. My wife Kitty and I were just married last winter. We wanted a truly memorable honeymoon, so we booked the cougar bow hunt offered by Extreme Honeymoons — it was available the week right after our wedding.

The professionals at Extreme Honeymoons handled every detail. Ted and Betty even gave us archery instruction before the trip. We met our guide, Tom Henry, who works with a team of four hound dogs. The hunt started after a light snow, making it easier to track the big cats. We spotted three cougars that day, though we didn't get a clean shot.

It was a tiring, exciting, and beautiful weekend. Thank you, Extreme Honeymoons, for creating such a special honeymoon memory. We're including a few snapshots from our great adventure.

Thanks for the memories,
Brian and Kitty Jones

Tribute #2 – David & Mary Farnsworth

We want to thank everyone at Extreme Honeymoons for an absolutely rip-roaring honeymoon!

I had always wanted to try heliskiing, and our honeymoon felt like the perfect time to go for it. Mary and I are experienced downhill skiers, but neither of us had done extreme skiing or snowboarding before. Your team of expert pilots and guides made the whole experience incredible.

We were blessed with fresh powder the weekend we went. Kings Peak — Utah's tallest mountain — was breathtaking, and we managed six runs a day, each one an adventure. Our equipment was thoroughly checked by the guides, so we had zero worries.

We'll be telling all our friends about this amazing way to start married life. Thank you for taking care of every detail so we could just enjoy the thrill together.

Yours truly,
David and Mary Farnsworth

Tribute #3 – Peter & Polly Renquist

Who would have thought you could spend your honeymoon having the adventure of a lifetime? I wanted to give my new wife Louise something truly exciting.

I've been mountain biking for about five years, and we're both in good shape, so the red rock adventure in Moab was exactly what we wanted. The bed and breakfast inn in Moab was wonderful. We enjoyed an early breakfast, then were transferred — along with our bikes, gear, and a light lunch — to the trailhead. We received a map and GPS, and off we went.

The day was hot (around 90 degrees), but we had plenty of water and never felt stressed. Every time we look at our photos from that weekend, we relive the wonderful time we had. The price was very reasonable, and we could never have pulled this off without your help.

Thanks for the time of our lives,
Peter and Polly Renquist
