Website Critiques

Questions

  1. What was your first impression of this site?
  2. How does this site organize the flow of information? (Do they have links on the left and text on the right? Some other scheme? No scheme?)
  3. Does the site structure make sense or does it confuse you? Why?
  4. Does the site graphics/layout/multimedia enhance the site or detract from it? Why?
  5. How long did it take for the home page to load? Was the load time reasonable?
  6. Was the text color and size readable? If a background color or image was used, did it enhance or detract from the site?
  7. Did the site have a navigation bar or other navigation tool? Was the navigation well done?
  8. Did the site have a color scheme or look and feel that was repeated on all the pages?
  9. How does the site respond to smaller window sizes (responsive design)?
  10. What does this site offer that was unusual or very well done?

Website 1 Critique Utah National Parks

www.utah.com

  1. My first impression was good, although the link brought me to a page that said "Oops! Couldn't find that."
  2. They have links on the top bar that had categories and you would click on a category and it would bring up more links
  3. Yes, to me the structure makes sense, it looks like other websites I have seen.
  4. I think that it enhances the experience. They chose a picture of it looks like arches national park and it gets me excited to see more
  5. The loading was almost instant to bring up the page
  6. Everything in the website enhanced the experience for me. The text was clear but the photos made it look colorful and fun.
  7. Yes it does have one at the top right, and yes it looks well done.
  8. I don't think it was repeated. Given the fact that every page had different photos of different places it did not repeat.
  9. It responds great to smaller sizes.
  10. I really liked all the colors and the pictures of national parks and how easy it was to find stuff in the top categories bar.
